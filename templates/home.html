Learn Discover Connect

The UW Web Service Registry provides a list of Web services that you can use and a place to advertise and communicate your Web service.

The registry is open to all UW people - it is intended as a public resource that serves two main goals. First, to allow UW people who maintain Web services to make them official - registration will provide services with a reserved namespace and make them visible to campus technology coordinators. Second, the registry allows UW people to browse for, locate and learn about applications that may be useful to them.

Looking for a service?

If you are looking for a source of data or functionality on campus, please browse the registry. You'll find information on what applications are availabe, who runs them, and how to use them.

Have a service to share?

Please register it! This is the easiest way to get exposure for your application, and the registry maintainers will help you with all the details.

Got an idea for a service?

Please add your idea and/or vote on other Web service ideas at uservoice! This is the place to get your voice heard on what Web services you need and watch us take action on your feedback.

Need help with the registry?

Give us a holla using our support email. wsregistry-support@u.washington.edu
